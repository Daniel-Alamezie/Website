About us...

Hello, we're C.E.M.D, a student led group based at Keele University. When the UK Government declared a "climate emergency" in 2019, Keele University was one of the first universities to join them and stand behind the urgency for change. We followed, creating a group with a vision; making the world a better place by encouraging younger generations to take care of the environment through data visulisation. Afterall, this planet is the only one we've got. Join us to hear more about Keele's efforts in creating a sustainable future.

Recycling and Waste Efforts


In 2010 Keele introduced a dual stream recycling system. Prior to this installation, there was no recycling on campus. Over the years, the recycling rate has increased to 72%. This is important information that deserves to be displayed in a well made graph.


In 2012 Keele built allotments in Lindsay Hall. A polytunnel was installed to lengthen the growing season, enabling Keele to produce loads of fresh produce. When this first began, the food was sold on campus to students and staff, however this unfortunately did not last. In addition to the Lindsay allotments, the Sustainable Student Bungalow was built in Barnes Hall. Four students are chosen to live here and manage the land. Food grown here has also been used to feed faculty, students, and even the Vice Chancellor. All profits from food sold are then used to buy more seeds.
